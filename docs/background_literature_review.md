# Bridging CD4⁺ T Cell Perturb-seq to Tumor Spatial Transcriptomics — Background Literature Review

*Prepared for the "Build From the Bench" project: connecting a genome-scale CD4⁺ T cell Perturb-seq atlas (Marson–Pritchard 2025) to tumor spatial transcriptomics (CosMx NSCLC) to prioritize, in an activation-context-specific manner, where T-cell-intrinsic regulators would act. This is a hypothesis-generation instrument, not a causal or predictive claim — the literature below is organized to show what each step of that pipeline stands on, and where the field's methods are still contested.*

---

## Scope and how to read this

The project chains three operations that come from three largely separate literatures: a pooled single-cell CRISPR screen (a **perturbation → response-vector** resource), a spatial-autocorrelation analysis of tissue (**where a program sits**), and a signature-alignment/projection step that welds the two. This review is built from a retrieved, verified reference set (77 primary papers spanning 2005–2026, DOIs resolved against PubMed and OpenAlex) and is organized by those methodological pillars rather than chronologically, because the design decisions the project faces — which perturbation resource, which spatial-variability statistic, which alignment metric, how to guard against artifacts — each map onto a specific, currently-active methods debate. Where a claim rests on a preprint it is flagged as such; where a method is contested, both sides are given.

---

## 1. The perturbation resource: Perturb-seq to context-resolved atlas

Pooled single-cell CRISPR screening was established almost simultaneously by four 2016–2017 papers that coupled a CRISPR library to a single-cell transcriptomic readout, so that each cell reports both which gene was perturbed and the genome-wide consequence. Perturb-seq itself introduced the scalable barcoded design and the analytical framing of a perturbation as a high-dimensional expression response ([Dixit 2016](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2016.11.038)); the multiplexed CRISPR-screening platform of ([Adamson 2016](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2016.11.048)) and the CROP-seq guide-capture strategy of ([Datlinger 2017](https://doi.org/10.1038/nmeth.4177)) solved the guide-assignment and library-complexity problems that made genome-scale screens feasible, building on the earlier demonstration that CRISPR-Cas9 could drive pooled loss-of-function screens in human cells ([Wang 2013](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1246981)). A recurring methodological thread — directly relevant to how this project consumes its input — is that raw single-cell CRISPR data are noisy and require an explicit statistical model to turn per-cell counts into a trustworthy per-perturbation effect; ([Duan 2019](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-019-10216-x)) formalized this as model-based estimation of perturbation effects, and the pseudobulk-DESeq2 pipeline the atlas uses is the current standard expression of that principle.

The field then scaled along two axes the project inherits. First, sheer size and readout richness: direct guide-capture enabled combinatorial screens ([Replogle 2020](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-020-0470-y)), and genome-scale Perturb-seq mapped information-rich genotype–phenotype landscapes across tens of thousands of perturbations ([Replogle 2022](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2022.05.013)) — the methodological template for a "perturbation × gene" effect matrix as an analyzable object in its own right. Transcriptome-wide differential-expression modeling across such perturbation atlases remains an active problem ([Nadig 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-025-02169-3)). Second, and more important for this project, the screen moved into primary human T cells and into physiologically meaningful contexts. Genome-wide CRISPR screens in primary human T cells first revealed that key proliferation and cytokine regulators are only discoverable in the correct stimulation context ([Shifrut 2018](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2018.10.024)); exhaustion-focused screens identified chromatin regulators of the dysfunctional state ([Belk 2022](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2022.06.001)); and epigenetic-editing and chromatin-remodeler screens have begun to resolve regulatory hierarchies rather than single genes ([Otto 2023](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.molcel.2023.03.013); [Pacalin 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-024-02213-3)).

The anchor dataset sits at the end of this trajectory. The Marson–Pritchard genome-scale CD4⁺ T cell Perturb-seq atlas perturbed the union of expressed genes and transcription factors across ~22M primary cells from four donors, in resting, 8-hour, and 48-hour restimulated conditions, and its central reported finding is precisely the axis this project hinges on: the active regulators and the gene programs they control change substantially with activation context ([Zhu 2025, bioRxiv](https://doi.org/10.64898/2025.12.23.696273)). Two consequences follow for the pipeline. The context-flip question — regulators whose effect direction reverses between rest and stimulation — is native to the data and is the atlas's own headline, not something the project imposes. And because the distributed `DE_stats` object already provides per-condition `log_fc`/`zscore` layers, the project can treat the atlas as a ready-made response-vector matrix and avoid reprocessing 22M cells, which is what makes a one-week scope feasible.

## 2. Perturbation-to-cell-state projection models

The project's third step — projecting a perturbation response vector onto observed tissue states — belongs to a fast-moving subfield on predicting or transferring perturbation effects across cellular contexts. The foundational demonstration that a latent generative model can predict a single-cell perturbation response in an unseen context is scGen ([Lotfollahi 2019](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-019-0494-8)); the complementary insight that perturbation responses live on a structured, navigable manifold — so that combinations and interpolations are meaningful — came from genetic-interaction-manifold work ([Norman 2019](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aax4438)). Subsequent models elaborated the generative approach (adversarial: [Wei 2022](https://doi.org/10.1093/bioinformatics/btac357); diffusion: [He 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-025-02877-y)) and, notably, began to separate *causal* perturbation effects from confounded correlation ([An 2025](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cels.2025.101443)), while a parallel effort works to interpret and decode the heterogeneity of responses across cells ([Song 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41556-025-01626-9)). Interpretable state↔perturbation correspondence models — the class the atlas's own `pert2state` belongs to — are exactly the reference implementation the project's transparent baseline should be compared against ([Xu 2024, bioRxiv](https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.03.14.585078)).

Two cautions from this literature should shape the project's claims. First, generalization is genuinely hard and often overstated: recent benchmarks show that many perturbation-response predictors fail to beat simple baselines out of distribution ([Wei 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-025-02980-0)), and a community prediction challenge was organized precisely because the field lacks a settled, honest performance baseline ([Zhang 2026, bioRxiv](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.05.21.726863)). This is the strongest external justification for the project's decision to make a transparent dot-product projection the deliverable and to treat learned models as an optional, clearly-labeled comparison. Second, the emerging foundation-model and multilayer-omics approaches ([Bai 2026](https://doi.org/10.1093/bioinformatics/btag307); [Chen 2026](https://doi.org/10.3389/fimmu.2026.1857447)) are promising but unproven for this specific transfer (dissociated in-vitro screen → in-situ tumor niche), and adopting one would import an evaluation burden the project cannot discharge in scope. The honest framing the plan already adopts — "the transcriptional program a knockout would push a CD4⁺ T cell toward," never "the knockout will remodel the niche" — is well-supported by how carefully this subfield itself hedges its transfer claims.

## 3. Spatial-autocorrelation statistics and their failure modes

Step 1 rests on spatial autocorrelation — the statistical formalization of "genes whose expression is non-randomly organized in space." The field has largely standardized on a small set of statistics (Moran's I, Geary's C, and Gaussian-process variants) and on mature implementations. SpatialDE introduced the Gaussian-process test for spatially variable genes ([Svensson 2018](https://doi.org/10.1038/nmeth.4636)); squidpy packaged Moran's I with permutation testing and the neighborhood-graph and niche-enrichment machinery the project needs for both program detection and niche definition ([Palla 2022](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-021-01358-2)); and Hotspot reframed the problem as autocorrelation-based *module* detection, which is attractive here because it yields coherent gene programs in one step rather than a ranked gene list ([DeTomaso 2021](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cels.2021.04.005)).

The critical message from the recent methods literature is that these tools **do not agree**, and the choice is not cosmetic. Multiple independent 2024–2025 benchmarks report substantial disagreement among spatially-variable-gene callers in both the genes they nominate and their false-discovery behavior ([Chen 2024](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13059-023-03145-y); [Chen 2025](https://doi.org/10.1093/bioinformatics/btaf131)), which is why the project's plan to report Moran's I *with permutation p-values* and to cross-check against a second method is the defensible posture rather than an optional nicety. Newer callers target specific weaknesses: PROST and DESpace improve power and domain-awareness ([Liang 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-44835-w); [Cai 2024](https://doi.org/10.1093/bioinformatics/btae027)), Bayesian and fused-coexpression models address spatial smoothing and coexpression structure ([Jiang 2022](https://doi.org/10.1002/sim.9530); [Seal 2025, bioRxiv](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.03.29.646124)), and — most relevant to this project's central confound — a distinct line of work identifies **cell-type-specific** spatially-variable genes, explicitly separating "a program is spatially organized" from "a cell type is spatially organized" ([Shang 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-56280-4)). That distinction is the single most important methodological guardrail for a T-cell-program analysis in mixed tissue, and it is the reason the project's use of a single-cell-resolution platform with per-cell `cell_type` labels (rather than spot-level data needing deconvolution) materially reduces risk. General reviews and practical guides map the remaining options ([Das 2024](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.csbj.2024.01.016); [SINFONIA, Jiang 2023](https://doi.org/10.3390/cells12040604); [Elhossiny 2026](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-0716-5027-1_12)).

## 4. Tumor spatial substrate: NSCLC T-cell architecture and TLS

The spatial half of the bridge needs a tissue in which organized T-cell programs demonstrably exist, and the NSCLC/immune spatial literature supplies both the substrate and a built-in positive control. Spatially resolved studies have established that T-cell state in tumors is spatially patterned rather than uniform: CD8 differentiation and dysfunction are governed by spatial position and niche ([Tooley 2022](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trecan.2022.04.003); [Li 2018](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2018.11.043)), TCR-and-transcriptome spatial maps reveal distinct clonal niches ([Liu 2022](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.immuni.2022.09.002)), and single-cell/spatial maps of exhaustion-associated microenvironments give reference annotations for immune neighborhoods ([Tietscher 2023](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-35238-w)). In NSCLC specifically, integrated single-cell-and-spatial analyses characterize the immune microenvironment and its myeloid and lymphoid compartments ([De 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-48700-8); [Larroquette 2022](https://doi.org/10.1136/jitc-2021-003890); [Zhu 2025](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2025.04.003)), and spatial signatures are increasingly tied to immunotherapy outcome ([Aung 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-025-02351-7)).

The cleanest test case for "a spatially organized T-cell program" is the tertiary lymphoid structure — an organized ectopic lymphoid aggregate whose very definition is spatial. Recent work shows that mature TLS evoke coordinated intratumoral T- and B-cell responses ([Li 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-59341-w)) and maps their spatial immune landscapes ([Xie 2025](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12916-025-03889-3)); the CosMx NSCLC dataset's pre-annotated "lymphoid structure" niche is therefore a principled positive control for step 1, not an arbitrary region. On the platform itself, systematic benchmarking of imaging-based spatial transcriptomics quantifies the sensitivity and gene-panel trade-offs of CosMx-class assays ([Wang 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-64990-y)) — directly relevant to the project's decision to compute alignment only on the ~500-gene shared space and to report that overlap with every score. Cell-neighborhood analysis frameworks provide the niche-delineation vocabulary the projection step will use ([Shimonov 2025, bioRxiv](https://doi.org/10.1101/2023.12.30.573739); [Lin 2023](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2022.12.028)).

## 5. Alignment, enrichment, and the deconvolution confound

The similarity computation at the heart of step 2 — scoring how well a perturbation's response vector matches a spatial program's gene weights — is a signature-enrichment or vector-correlation operation with well-understood tooling. Gene-set enrichment analysis remains the reference method for asking whether a gene set is coherently up- or down-regulated within a ranked response ([Subramanian 2005](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0506580102)), and modern ensemble frameworks such as decoupleR make it straightforward to run enrichment and vector-based activity scoring consistently across the shared gene space ([Badia-i-Mompel 2022](https://doi.org/10.1093/bioadv/vbac016)). The methodological point the project must respect is circularity: if the genes that define the spatial program also dominate the perturbation signature, the alignment inflates trivially — hence the plan's permutation and spatial-rotation nulls and per-(program, state) FDR, which are the standard defenses in this setting.

The largest residual risk is cell-type confounding, and this is where the deconvolution literature is both a resource and a warning. Reference-based deconvolution methods (cell2location, Tangram, and the broader integration ecosystem) estimate cell-type composition per spot and are the standard way to separate "T-cell state" from "T-cell abundance" ([Kleshchevnikov 2022](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-021-01139-4); [Biancalani 2021](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-021-01264-7)). But independent benchmarks show these methods disagree substantially and that no single deconvolution or integration method dominates ([Li 2022](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-022-01480-9); [Yan 2023](https://doi.org/10.1093/bioinformatics/btac805); [Sang-Aram 2024](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.88431); [Slabowska 2024](https://doi.org/10.3389/fbinf.2024.1352594)) — so a pipeline whose top hits depend on a particular deconvolution choice is fragile. This is a second, independent argument for the single-cell-resolution route: with per-cell `cell_type` labels the project can restrict directly to CD4 memory/naive and Treg cells and largely sidestep the deconvolution-method lottery, converting the confound from a modeling problem into a filtering step.

## 6. Direct precedents coupling perturbation and space

Two developments show the project's core idea is timely rather than idiosyncratic, and both should be cited as the nearest prior art. First, perturbation screening and spatial readout are now being fused *experimentally*: simultaneous CRISPR screening with spatial transcriptomics resolves where perturbations act within tissue architecture ([Binan 2025](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2025.02.012)), and massively parallel in-vivo Perturb-seq reads out cell-type-specific perturbation effects in intact tissue ([Zheng 2024](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2024.04.050); protocol: [Zheng 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41596-024-01119-3)). These define the experimental frontier that this project's *computational* bridge is a low-cost, hypothesis-generating stand-in for — and the contrast sharpens the honest framing: an in-silico alignment prioritizes candidates that such an experiment would then test. Second, atlas-guided in-silico nomination of T-cell-programming transcription factors ([Chung 2025, bioRxiv](https://doi.org/10.1101/2023.01.03.522354)) is a close methodological sibling: it uses a perturbation/expression atlas to rank regulators for a desired cell state, which is structurally the same prioritization the project performs, redirected onto spatial programs.

## 7. Synthesis and the honest bottom line

The pipeline is well-grounded step by step: the perturbation resource is a mature, context-resolved atlas whose own central result licenses the activation-context axis; the spatial-autocorrelation step uses standard, benchmarked statistics; the alignment step is a legitimate enrichment/correlation computation with established null-model defenses; and the projection step has a direct in-house precedent and a cautionary benchmark literature that argues for a transparent baseline over a learned model. The two genuine methodological hazards are both named repeatedly in the primary literature and both are mitigated by the project's dataset choice rather than by a modeling trick: **cell-type confounding** (addressed by single-cell resolution + per-cell labels + cell-type-specific SVG awareness) and **unstable alignment on a small shared gene space** (addressed by reporting overlap per score and down-weighting thin-support programs). The literature offers no support for reading any output as evidence that a knockout remodels a niche — the transfer gap from a dissociated cytokine-driven monoculture to an in-situ tumor is real and unbridged — so the plan's insistence on "exploratory projection, not experimentally validated" labeling is not boilerplate but the position the field's own hedging demands. Confidence that a clean, reproducible, honestly-scoped prioritization instrument is achievable: high. Confidence that any specific top-ranked regulator is a real niche remodeler: low by construction — which is the intended downstream experiment, and, per §6, one the field is now equipped to run.
